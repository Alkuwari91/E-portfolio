Unit 1: Introduction to Machine Learning (ML)

Welcome to Week 1 of the Machine Learning Course! In this unit, we will explore the fundamental concepts of machine learning (ML), an area of artificial intelligence (AI) that empowers systems to learn from data and make decisions with minimal human intervention.

Machine learning has become an integral part of our daily life. From browsing the internet to getting a bank loan, machine learning is used to make decisions and for profiling. The availability of big data on a real-time basis facilitates the learning of the ML algorithms, which in turn provides machines with the ability to make more accurate decisions autonomously (Schwab & Zahid, 2020).

What is Machine Learning?

Machine learning is the study of algorithms that enable computers to perform tasks without explicit instructions. These algorithms are trained on data and can automatically improve their performance over time.

This week we shall learn about:

  • The past, present, and future of machine learning.
  • The challenges and opportunities of using algorithms.
  • The interplay among big data analytics, machine learning, and artificial intelligence.

On completion of this week, you will be able to:

  • Have a better understanding of the role of machine learning in future industries.
  • Identify skill sets required to become proficient in machine learning.
  • Know about the pitfalls of machine learning and ways to address them.
